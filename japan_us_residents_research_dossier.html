U.S. Citizens & U.S.-Born Residents in Japan

01

データ公開機関

出入国在留管理庁
Immigration Services Agency of Japan (ISA)

Publishes the 在留外国人統計 (Statistics on Foreign Residents), broken down by nationality, status of residence, prefecture, and age. Primary source for U.S. national counts in Japan.

moj.go.jp — 在留外国人統計
総務省統計局
Statistics Bureau of Japan (SBJ)

Conducts the 国勢調査 (National Census, every 5 years). Includes country-of-birth data for residents. Supplemented by 人口推計 (Population Estimates, monthly/annual).

stat.go.jp — 国勢調査
stat.go.jp — トップ
e-Stat（政府統計の総合窓口）
e-Stat — Portal for Japanese Government Statistics

Aggregated portal hosting machine-readable datasets from ISA, SBJ, and other ministries. Allows CSV/Excel download and API access. Primary tool for data retrieval.

e-stat.go.jp
外務省
Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MOFA)

Publishes 海外在留邦人数調査統計 (statistics on Japanese nationals abroad) — relevant for cross-referencing U.S.–Japan bilateral flows. Does not publish inbound U.S. resident counts directly.

mofa.go.jp — 海外在留邦人数

03

現地での定義・用語

在留外国人 Zairyū Gaikokujin
Foreign nationals holding a valid 在留カード (Residence Card) or 特別永住者証明書 (Special Permanent Resident Certificate) and registered in Japan's residency system. Excludes short-stay tourists and holders of short-term visas (90 days or less). This is the primary unit of count in ISA statistics.
国籍 vs 出生地 Nationality vs. Country of Birth
ISA statistics classify by nationality (国籍). The National Census additionally captures country of birth (出生国), making it the only source allowing identification of U.S.-born individuals who may hold non-U.S. passports (e.g., naturalized Japanese citizens born in the U.S.).
在留資格 Status of Residence
Legal category under which a foreigner resides in Japan. ISA data disaggregates U.S. nationals by status (e.g., 永住者 Permanent Resident, 技術・人文知識・国際業務 Engineer/Specialist, 日本人の配偶者等 Spouse of Japanese National, 留学 Student, etc.).
04

在留・市民権の取得経路（米国市民 → 日本）

1

Entry visa → Status of Residence: U.S. citizens enter Japan on a temporary visitor exemption (最大90日) or apply for a specific 在留資格 visa before entry. Upon arrival, they are issued a Residence Card if staying longer than 3 months.

2

Resident Registration (住民登録): Those staying more than 3 months must register at their local municipal office (市区町村). This is the point at which they enter population databases linked to the 住民基本台帳.

3

Permanent Residency (永住許可): Requires generally 10 years of continuous residence (with 5 years on a working visa), stable income, and good conduct. Spouses of Japanese nationals may qualify after 3 years. U.S. nationals are among the top nationalities holding this status.

4

Naturalization (帰化): Requires 5+ years of continuous residence, renunciation of prior citizenship (Japan does not legally recognize dual nationality for adults), and language/civics criteria. Naturalized citizens exit all foreign national statistics and appear only in census country-of-birth data.

※ Pathway rules as of 2026. Immigration law amendments should be verified via the ISA official site.

05

データの空白・制限事項

Gap 01 — No Direct "U.S. Citizen" Count ISA tracks nationality of passport, not citizenship. A U.S.-born person who has naturalized to another nationality will not appear in the "U.S." category. Dual nationals are classified by the nationality declared at registration.
Gap 02 — Short-Stay Visitors Excluded Neither ISA nor census data covers U.S. nationals on tourist or short-term visas (under 90 days). Japan Tourism Agency (観光庁) publishes entry/exit statistics by nationality, but these count trips, not individuals.
Gap 03 — Census Periodicity Country-of-birth data is only available every 5 years (last: 2020, next: 2025 results expected ~2026). For intercensal years, nationality-based ISA data is the only available proxy.
Gap 04 — U.S. Military Personnel U.S. military personnel and their dependents stationed in Japan under the SOFA agreement are generally not counted in ISA resident statistics. The U.S. Department of Defense publishes its own count separately, not through Japanese government sources.
Gap 05 — Undocumented / Overstay Population ISA separately publishes overstay (不法残留者) estimates annually, disaggregated by nationality. However, these are estimates and the U.S. figure is typically small relative to total overstay numbers.
